Johan Cruyff

The man who defined modern football

Some of his biggest legacy:

  • Throughout his career, Cruyff became synonymous with the playing style of "Total Football".
  • Cruyff always considered the aesthetic and moral aspects of the game; it was not just about winning, but about winning with the "right" style and in the "right" way.
  • Cruyff is widely seen as a revolutionary figure in the history of Ajax, Barcelona, and the Netherlands.
  • The style of play Cruyff introduced at Barcelona later came to be known as tiki-taka and positional play — characterised by short passing and movement, working the ball through various channels, and maintaining possession.
  • This style of play went on to influence the world of football today, with most of the teams apply many characteristics into their gameplan.
  • The biggest managers that are influenced by positional play include Pep Guardiola, Nagelsmann, Xabi Alonso, Xavi Hernandez, Mikel Arteta, etc.

Some of his biggest achievements:

  • As a Player:
    • Ballon d'Or: 1971, 1973, 1974.
    • FIFA World Cup Golden Ball: 1974.
    • World Soccer's The Greatest Players of the 20th Century: #3.
    • IFFHS European Player of the Century: #1.
  • As a Manager:
    • World Soccer Awards Manager of the Year: 1987.
    • Don Balón Award for Coach of the Year: 1991, 1992.
    • El País Manager of the Year: 1992, 1993, 1994.
    • France Football Greatest Manager of All Time: #4 (2019)

“Without him there would have been no Messi, Xavi, Iniesta,” said Albert Ferrer, while Xavi added: “Cruyff is the father of everything, the patriarch of this philosophy, the DNA of Barça.”

-- A tribute to Johan Cruyff, InsideFIFA
